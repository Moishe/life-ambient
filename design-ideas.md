Some high level thoughts:

* when a new cell is born, it makes a gentle, low volume but maybe high-attack tone. Like a very muted cymbal or triangle or xylophone
* as a cell stays alive, it produces an ambient pad sound. Clusters of neighboring cells create on ambient pad whose tone is modulated by the size and shape of the cluster. It could be that a growing "cluster" of cells is defined by the shape of the bounding polygon or something like that (so it wouldn't need to be a contiguous group of all-alive cells, because Life's algorithm precludes that). For instance, a "blinker" may warble.
* a group's position can determine its pitch, so a glider might warble as it descends in pitch.
* this should be interactive, ideally in the browser
* the user should be able to select the key in which the music is produced
